# 09 — Methodology & Evidence Base

All numerical defaults trace to cited sources. When evidence is mixed,
the more conservative value is used.

## Nutrition hierarchy

The [Nutrition Pyramid](https://rippedbody.com/nutrition-pyramid-overview/):
1. **Calories** — energy balance is the single most important factor.
2. **Macros** — protein, fat, carb partitioning.
3. **Micros** — vitamins, minerals, fibre, water.
4. **Nutrient timing** — minor impact.
5. **Supplements** — least important layer.

## Calorie methodology

Source: [rippedbody.com/calories](https://rippedbody.com/calories/)

- **BMR:** Harris-Benedict (metric).
- **TDEE:** 4-tier activity multipliers (1.15-1.75).
- **Fat loss:** 0.75% BW/week (accounts for metabolic adaptation; the
  15-25% TDEE drop during prolonged restriction is pre-buffered).
- **Muscle gain:** Experience-tiered monthly rates (2%/1%/0.5% BW).
  Surplus includes a 50% NEAT buffer.
- **Recomp:** Maintenance calories.

Source: [updated-bulking-guidelines](https://rippedbody.com/updated-bulking-guidelines/),
[how-to-bulk](https://rippedbody.com/how-to-bulk/).

## Macro methodology

Source: [rippedbody.com/macros](https://rippedbody.com/macros/),
[best-macro-ratio](https://rippedbody.com/best-macro-ratio/)

There is no "best macro ratio." Protein is set by lean mass (or body
weight if BF% unknown), fat is 15-30% of calories, carbs fill the rest.
The ratio changes as you diet or bulk because calorie needs change but
protein needs do not.

**Vegan protein** is set higher due to lower bioavailability and BCAA
content: [advice-for-vegans](https://rippedbody.com/advice-for-vegans/).

## Body-fat estimation

Sources: [body-fat-guide](https://rippedbody.com/body-fat-guide/),
[how-calculate-body-fat-percentage](https://rippedbody.com/how-calculate-body-fat-percentage/)

Priority: user input → Navy tape → visual estimation → Deurenberg BMI
method. Most people underestimate BF by ~50% (RippedBody corrects
self-estimates).

## Somatotype

Source: [muscleandstrength.com](https://www.muscleandstrength.com/articles/body-types-ectomorph-mesomorph-endomorph.html)

3 types (ecto/meso/endo), inferred from BMI + body fat, refined by
frame size (wrist circumference).

## Muscular potential

Source: [maximum-muscular-potential](https://rippedbody.com/maximum-muscular-potential/)

Berkhan model: stage-shredded max = height_cm − 100.
FFMI ceiling for naturals: ~25 (Kouri et al. 1995).

## Training methodology

Source: [how-to-build-training-programs](https://rippedbody.com/how-to-build-training-programs/)

Based on the Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Training (Helms et al.).
Splits, volume, intensity (RIR), progression, and exercise selection
follow the Pyramid's step-by-step framework.

## Adjustments

Sources: [how-to-adjust-macros](https://rippedbody.com/how-to-adjust-macros/),
[how-to-adjust-macros-bulk](https://rippedbody.com/how-to-adjust-macros-bulk/),
[training-plateaus](https://rippedbody.com/training-plateaus/)

Key principle: calorie adjustment is the **last resort**. Work through
tracking accuracy, hunger/environment management, stress, sleep, NEAT,
and patience before changing intake.

## Limitations

- All calculations are **estimations** — individuals vary ±15% from
  BMR formulas. Track progress and adjust.
- Somatotype is a heuristic, not a fixed taxonomy.
- Body-fat estimation methods all have large individual error.
- The system does not account for medical conditions, medications, or
  pregnancy — consult a physician.
