Dumbbell & Barbell Mass Workout

Written By: M&S Writers
September 16th, 2020
Updated: April 1st, 2021
Categories: Workouts Muscle Building
2.8M Reads

Looking to add muscle mass? Using only dumbbells and barbells, this workout hits each muscle group hard once a week over a 5 day schedule.
Workout Summary
  • Main Goal
    Build Muscle
  • Workout Type
    Split
  • Training Level
    Intermediate
  • Program Duration8 weeks
  • Days Per Week
    5
  • Time Per Workout65 minutes
  • Equipment Required
    Barbell, Bodyweight, Dumbbells
  • Target Gender Male & Female
  • Recommended Supps
    Protein Powder
    Creatine Monohydrate
    Multivitamin
    Essential Fats (EFAs)
    Weight Gainer (Optional)
  • Workout PDF Download Workout

Workout Description

Looking to add muscle mass? Maybe this is the right workout for you. Using only dumbbells and barbells, this workout hits each muscle group hard once a week over a 5 day schedule. This workout is intense, so you will get the best results if you supplement with creatine and protein. Remember to get plenty of rest and minimize cardio while you're trying to bulk up. Good luck.

Workout Schedule

  • Monday - Shoulders
  • Tuesday - Back
  • Wednesday - Chest, Triceps, and Abs
  • Thursday - Biceps
  • Friday - Legs
  • Saturday - Rest
  • Sunday - Rest

Dumbbell & Barbell Mass Workout

Monday - Shoulders

Exercise Sets Reps
Military Press 4 10-14
Seated Dumbbell Press 4 8
Dumbbell Front Raise 3 8-10
Dumbbell Lateral Raise 5 12, 10, 8, 8, 6
Dumbbell Reverse Fly 3 10

Tuesday - Back

Exercise Sets Reps
Wide Grip Pull Up 7 12 down to 6
Bent Over Barbell Row 4 12
One Arm Dumbbell Row 3 10
Deadlifts 4 10
Dumbbell Shrugs 4 8-12

Wednesday - Chest, Triceps and Abs

Exercise Sets Reps
Barbell Bench Press 4 8
Incline Dumbbell Bench Press 4 10
Decline Dumbbell Bench Press 3 8
Dumbbell Flys 4 12
Chest Dip 4 MAX (weighted)
Close Grip Bench Press 5 5
Tricep Kickback 3 12-15 light
One Arm Dumbbell Extension 3 8-10
Lying Tricep Extension 4 12 strict
Decline Sit Ups 4 MAX

Thursday - Biceps

Exercise Sets Reps
Standing Barbell Curl 4 8
Preacher Curl 4 8
Standing Hammer Curl 4 10
Concentration Curl 3 12
Reverse Barbell Curl 3 7-8

Friday - Legs

Exercise Sets Reps
Dumbbell Lunge 4 5 each leg
Squat 6 10
Stiff Leg Deadlifts 4 10
Standing Calf Raise 8 15-20

Doug
Posted on: Sat, 06/15/2024 - 19:23

I dread reaching complete failure on the reps because i work out at home alone. How best should i manage this so i can achieve maximum results?

  • Reply
  • reply
Roger
Posted on: Tue, 06/18/2024 - 08:29

I train at home alone as well, Doug. Here is what I do.

If you are using a barbell on a bench, then when you approach failure, stop on the last positive rep and hold it in that static position for as long as you can before racking it. Don't do any negatives and risk getting stuck.

If you are using a barbell without a bench and not doing a squat (curl, row, etc.), then you can do cheat reps where you use a little momentum to keep it going, then do a negative. Once you feel you have to use a lot of momentum, just stop. If you are doing squats, just rack it when you think you are approaching failure. Not worth it to push it beyond that.

With dumbbells, end the set with a static hold followed by a negative. You should be able to let the weights go without breaking them. I hope these tips help.
